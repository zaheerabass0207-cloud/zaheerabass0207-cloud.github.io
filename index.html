About Me

Academic background & overview

I am a PhD Scholar in the Department of Civil Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati (IIT Guwahati), working under the supervision of Dr. Vivek Padmanabha . at Shock Wave and Impact Research Laboratory (SWIRL). My doctoral research is focused on the dynamic strength and fragmentation analysis of brittlr geomaterials, with a particular emphasis on rock mechanics and geomechanics.

My research aims to characterise the mechanical response of brittle synthetic and natural geo-materials emphasied on Rock under dynamic loading conditions, investigate fragmentation behaviour, and develop insights applicable to Blasting, underground excavation, slope stability, and earthquake-induced failures in challenging geological terrains.

Prior to joining IIT Guwahati, I completed my M.Tech. in Geotechnical Engineering from the National Institute of Technology Silchar (NIT Silchar) with a CGPA of 8.9/10. My published work includes studies on rock slope stability and seismic vulnerability assessment along the NH-1 Highway in Ladakh.

I am passionate about combining experimental investigations with numerical modelling tools to solve complex geotechnical and geo-mechanical problems, especially in the context of high-strain loading (under shock and impact) conditions.

Research Interests

Core areas of academic focus

Rock Mechanics

Mechanical behaviour of rocks under static and dynamic loading; failure criteria and constitutive modelling.

Dynamic Strength of Geomaterials

High strain-rate response of rocks and geo-materials; rate-dependent failure mechanisms.

Fragmentation Analysis

Size distribution and energy dissipation during dynamic fragmentation of rock specimens.

Geomechanics

Stress-deformation behaviour of geo-materials; underground excavation and tunnel stability.

Rock Slope Stability

Kinematic and limit-equilibrium analysis of rock slopes; seismically triggered failures in mountainous terrain.

Seismic Vulnerability Assessment

Rock mass classification and seismic slope stability analysis along highway corridors.

Numerical Modelling

FEM/FDM simulation of geotechnical problems using RS2, RS3, Plaxis, and Abaqus.

Highway Geotechnics

Geotechnical investigations and hazard assessment for infrastructure in high-altitude and seismic zones.

Projects & Research Work

Ongoing doctoral research and major academic work

Dynamic Strength & Fragmentation of Geomaterials

Ongoing – PhD Research

Doctoral research focused on characterising the dynamic mechanical behaviour and fragmentation patterns of natural and synthetic brittle geomaterials, emphasizing Rock under high-strain-rate loading. Investigating energy dissipation, crack propagation, and size and shape distribution of fragments, practically applicable to excavation and blasting applications. Under the supervision of Dr. Vivek Padmanabha .

Rock Mechanics Dynamic Loading Fragmentation Geomechanics

Rock Slope Stability – NH-1 Ladakh

Completed

Comprehensive stability analysis of rock slopes along National Highway 1 in Ladakh using kinematic analysis, rock mass classification (SMR, RMR, Q-system), and limit-equilibrium methods. Results published as a book chapter in LNCE (2025).

Slope Stability NH-1 Ladakh RMR / SMR Kinematic Analysis

Seismic Vulnerability of Highway Slopes

Completed

Seismic slope stability assessment along NH-1, Ladakh, combining rock mass classification systems with pseudo-static and dynamic seismic analysis. Identified critical failure zones and proposed mitigation strategies. Published in LNCE (2026) in collaboration with Dr. Vivek Padmanabha .
